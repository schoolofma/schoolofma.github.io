Making Sense of Empathy

  • / 20 April - 22 May 2015

  • / five weeks, full-time

  • / € 1600* (until 20th Feb--see FAQ)

How can we create meaningful experiences using technology to express who we are and where we came from? This course will focus on Empathy and Culture in the context of art and technology. While learning digital fabrication techniques, designing mechanisms and kinetic sculptures as a way to animate the invisible forces which shape our human behaviour, we’ll simultaneously explore Empathy as a necessary tool to strengthen human connection and enhance our experiences of life.

The goal of the program is to explore in-depth the creative and expressive possibilities of digital fabrication (3D printers, CNC) and kinectic objects while delving into speculative critical design. In addition, we’ll also have an opportunity to explore Oculus Rift and ways in which it helps us to extend our empathic capabilities outside ourselves. Teachers and students will work together towards the production of individual or group projects and will have access to the equipment needed to create functioning kinectic installations. Expect to work with your hands, play with machinery, electronics, and code, to be experimental and have fun!
