APPLY


The course will focus on a critical exploration of the Internet as a transformative social environmental medium. The program will look deeply into the aesthetic narrative proposed by the net in order to understand the real impact of communication technologies in our society and the physical world.

The main purpose of the program is to provide students with theoretical background and technical skills to creatively intervene in the aesthetic assembly of web interfaces in order to break, redefine and reappropriate them - an urgent exercise to build technological paradigms which can more meaningfully respond to our current environmental and human conditions.

Course Description

This programme seeks to critically reveal, analyze and connect the complex network of agents that converge on the configuration of the Graphic User Interface (GUI). During a 4 week-long programme we will creatively reveal, deconstruct and re-articulate the material and immaterial elements that construct the GUI in order to reflect on its role and influence within the social, political, economic, cultural and emotional spheres of the networked society.

The main purpose of the program is to provide the students with theoretical background and technical skills to explore, develop and test disruptive methodologies in the generation of critical thought about the true nature of technology, and in the imagination of alternative techno-paradigms which may coherently respond to our environmental and human conditions.

Who is this program for?

This program is for artists, designers, writers, makers, coders, journalists and researchers interested in understanding, exploring and intervening (disrupting) what's behind web interfaces and acknowledging the social, political, economical, cultural and emotional effects of the Interface in the networked society.

What you will learn

  • HTML for web page layout

  • CSS for stylising web design

  • Javascript + JQuery for interactivity

  • Python as it relates to text-based machine learning

  • Code & Language: Algorithms & Politics, Code & Emotions

  • Interface & Ideology: Commodification of data, Governance, Social Engineering

  • The physical layer of the Interface: the physical structure of the Internet, Geopolitics and CO2 emissions

  • Interface hacks: art, hacktivism

Instructors

  • Joana Moll
    / janavirgin.com

    Joana Moll is a Barcelona based artist and a researcher. Her work critically explores the way post-capitalist narratives affect the alphabetization of machines, humans and ecosystems. Her main research topics include communication technologies and CO2 emissions, virtual civil surveillance on the Internet and language.

    She is a member of the scientific and artistic committee of the transdisciplinary research project Antiatlas des Frontières and co-founder of The Institute for the Advancement of Popular Automatisms. She is a visiting lecturer at College of Art and Design of Vic and a lecturer at VIT Lab in Vic (Barcelona).

  • Ben grosser / bengrosser.com/

    Artist Ben Grosser focuses on the cultural, social, and political implications of software. Recent exhibition venues include Eyebeam in New York, The White Building in London, Galerie Charlot in Paris, and Museum Ludwig in Cologne. His works have been featured in Wired, The Atlantic, The Guardian, Al Jazeera, and Der Spiegel. The Chicago Tribune called him the “unrivaled king of ominous gibberish.” Slate referred to his work as “creative civil disobedience in the digital age.”

    Grosser’s recognitions include First Prize in VIDA 16, the Expanded Media Award for Network Culture from Stuttgarter Filmwinter, and a Rhizome commission. He is an assistant professor at the University of Illinois and a faculty affiliate at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications. Prior to his current appointments, he earned an MFA in new media and an MM in music composition (both from Illinois), and was director of the Imaging Technology Group at the Beckman Institute.

    Bani Brusadin is a free lance producer and researcher in the troubled waters where contemporary art, networked technologies, popular cultures and politics meet, and often times clash. In the past he has been involved in different art and activist projects and since 2004, together with Eva & Franco Mattes, he co-directs "The Influencers", a festival about non conventional forms of art and communication. Bani also teaches about digital cultures and social change at the University of Barcelona, where he is also a PhD candidate in Advanced Studies in Art Production.
