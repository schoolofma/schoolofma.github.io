The School sounds like a perfect fit for me! How does the admissions process work?

Lovely! The admissions process works like this: Once you've applied to School of MA, your application will be reviewed within a few days. You'll receive an e-mail from us and we'll schedule an online call within a week. The call is a chance for us to get to know each other and for you to ask questions about the program. Soon after, you'll know if you're in!

If so, we'll then ask that you transfer a small deposit fee to us (30% of program cost) within one week to confirm your participation. Pretty standard procedure, really.

This rolling admissions process is intended to make it easier for those of you travelling from abroad and needing to make plans such as travel and visa arrangements asap!

What kind of students are you looking for?

The curious and inquisitive kind, creative people or the kind of people who yearn to be creative. We, also really like nice people and those who enjoy helping others and sharing knowledge. Hi-five if this sounds like you!

More specifically, we'd love to find people ready to experiment, to dream up new ideas and to learn the skills to make these ideas come to life. Technology has been advancing quickly. The things we're able to learn now weren't available back in the day. We're excited for people who've already started down a path that no longer fits them to join us, and push their own unique, creative ideas forward into the future.

In short, openness, the ability to work well with others, enthusiasm, and positivity are all traits we look for in potential students.

You say programs are full-time, what does that mean exactly?

Our four week programs are full-time which means they run from approximately 10am--7pm each day, with an hour break for lunch. Sometimes we'll have guest speakers and in the afternoons you can take advantage of open lab time.

Lately, we've been encouraging students to use fridays as a chance to share knowledge and skills with each other and it's been brilliant.

In general, our programs are a mix of lectures, labs, artist talks, field trips, plus lots of time to experiment and create. It's intensive but will probably be one of the most freeing, creative, and productive times of your life. How lucky for you to have found us!

Do I need to have prior experience before signing up?

No, but you can! For most of our programs, we don't expect any prior knowledge but if you do have some, you may be able to push yourself further much faster. We have some really great instructors and no doubt you will learn a lot from them and from the other students, regardless of your current skill level. This is not about being the smartest, it's about learning to expose your creative innards to the world in your own unique way and working with new tools and people who can help make it happen.

Where are your courses based?

Our upcoming spring and summer programs, which we are taking applications will all be taking place in Berlin, Germany, our home base. We love to take our programs abroad through, and have a course running in Shanghai and Shenzhen, China, this year and also one in Galway, Ireland!

What will I take out of a 4-week course?

We would like our students to leave the month long courses activated, not only equipped with technical and hands-on tools and skill sets, but also, as more critically-minded and engaged in their local communities and society at large. By commiting to a four week program, you will create space for meaningful engagement with the technologies we will work with, and you will have time for deeper interactions with your teachers and colleagues. There will definitely be ocassions to work collaboratively and learn from one another.

A four week program allows time for expanding your fields of knowledge and build towards a more interdisciplinary practice. We also encourage students without any previous experience to apply, as we believe in more diverse tech communities and in the formative qualities of what makes us each distinctive humans, our unique experiences, beliefs and attitudes.

Do you offer assistance or scholarships

Currently, our scholarship applications are closed. This year we offered 21 full tuition scholarships for our 2019 programs. We put out a call for all scholarships in December so that we can have enough time to review everyones applications, and let recipients know in good time so that they can make travel and accomodations arrangements.

What we can offer students who've been accepted into one of our courses is a letter of acceptance which can aid in securing grants to help cover the cost of the program. We cannot secure these grants for you, but we definitely encourage you to seek out funding as many previous students have been able to in the past! Key words to look out for: travel grants, government and artist grants, study abroad funding. If you're in a university, you're highly encouraged to ask your administrators if they cover these kinds of costs! Many do!

Can I attend part-time?

Most of our programs do require a full-time commitment and we're looking for people who can dedicate themselves to it. We've all taken two day courses and shorter programs. If we learn something but don't have time to use those skills, we easily forget.

At present, we allow a part-time (2 week) commitment option for the first two weeks of the Data and Society program only.

If you have to do some part time work on the side, we completely understand. It's just that you'll be able to get more out of the program if you're able to set your work aside for evenings and weekends. We believe in full-immersion so that the new things you learn will stick with you. Our hope for you is that once you leave, you'll be armed with a new concrete toolset and can quite easily dream up new ideas and actually know how to turn those ideas into tangible reality.

Do you help in finding and securing accomodation in Berlin?

Generally what we do is put the word out to our community to see who has rooms available for rent and that gets us pretty far! At the same time, it's good for you to also be on the lookout that way our chances are better to ensure total housing success! Ha! Most often when we hear about something we put students in touch with people who are renting directly so they can work out the fine details. Rent in Berlin (for a month) generally ranges between €350-€550, depending on location, size, etc. Sometimes you'll find something more expensive (and generally super nice!) but final decisions for what housing you decide on are up to you!

What's your cancellation policy?

Students send in 30% of the program fee as a deposit within one week of being accepted in a program. Full fee is due a week before class begins. You may cancel anytime before class begins and receive a refund less 50% of your deposit. Note: No refunds once class has begun.

Do you help with obtaining visas?

If you're selected to attend the School, we can send you an official letter of acceptance, but we don't have the capacity to help out much more than that. That being said, if you will require a visa, send us an email and we'll make reviewing your application a priority so do get in touch: info@schoolofma.org

What does tuition cover?

Tuition covers tuition fee, in-class material for class exercises, use of space and professional mentorship.

What does tuition NOT cover?

The tuition does NOT cover accomodation or travel costs.

Out of curiosity, how is the school funded?

Currently we're funded solely through student fees. All the money we receive goes entirely back into the school to pay for our facilities, brilliant instructors, admin, taxes, etc.

This is a grassroots program driven only by passion and a belief in the power of art, technology, and education as an inevitable force for creating positive social change. While we do have a fiscal sponsor, OpenTechSchool e.V., we're currently in the process of establishing ourselves as a legal non-profit entity.

Cool. Any final words on finances?

We've tried to keep the costs down as much as humanly possible! That being said, you're not just paying for classes. The money is being used for materials and to allow an amazing community of people to come together, all of whom are passionate about dreaming up ideas and seeing how far they can push the technology, the creativity, and themselves. It's a unique opportunity to collaborate with and learn from everyone participating and to create something amazing from nothing. Even in a parallel universe I think it would cost just as much, if not more than what we're charging! But good luck getting a cheap ticket to that place.

We've had many great instructors come through and quite a few of them are doing important work in these areas.
