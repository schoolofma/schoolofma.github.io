FAQ

If you have a question and don't see it answered here, send us an email and we can add it to this section.

The School sounds like a perfect fit for me! How much does it cost?

Tuition is €1600 (early bird--apply by 27th February) for the full-time, five-week program in Belgrade, Serbia. Applying afterwards? The price is €1900.

What does the cost cover?

The money we receive will go entirely back into the school to pay for our amazing facility (UKParabrod), brilliant instructors, admin, taxes, etc. We will also cover the cost of basic materials and components for the classroom, as much as we can anyway, until the money runs out!

What does the tuition NOT cover?

The tuition does not cover housing or travel to/from Belgrade. It is a relatively inexpensive city, though. Even just looking on Airbnb you'll find rooms for €15/night and monthly rent from €300. We can also help look into group housing options if some of you would like to share flats while you're here.

Cool. Anything else you can say to convince me?

We've tried to keep the costs down as much as humanly possible! In an attempt to be transparent, we will be posting all of our finances online. That being said, you're not just paying for classes. The money is being used to allow an amazing community of people to come together, all of whom are passionate about dreaming up ideas and seeing how far they can push the technology, the art and themselves. It's a unique opportunity to collaborate with and learn from everyone participating and to create something amazing from nothing. Even in a parallel universe I think it would cost €1600, if not more! But good luck getting a cheap ticket to that place.

What about sponsors?

If we find paying sponsors and all other costs are covered, we will bring the price of tuition down or refund some money. That being said, if you know of any glorious people willing to help fund us — by all means — give them my number! +49 151 753 74 602

Thanks for answering my money questions, now about the School. Can I attend part-time?

The program requires a full-time commitment and we are looking for people who can dedicate the full five weeks to it.

What kind of students are you looking for?

The curious and inquisitive kind, creative people or the kind of people who yearn to be creative. We, also really like nice people and those who enjoy helping others and sharing knowledge. Hi-five if this sounds like you!

Do I need to know how to program or build circuits before signing up?

No, but you can! We don't expect any prior knowledge but if you do have some, you may be able to push yourself further much faster. We have some really great instructors and no doubt you will learn a lot from them and from the other students, regardless of your current skill level. This is not about being the smartest, it's about learning to expose your creative innards to the world in your own unique way and working with new tools and people who can help make it happen.

You say the program is full-time, what does that mean exactly?

Most days we will have a morning check-in beginning at 10am, and then a morning class 'til around lunchtime. Some days there will be a class or a talk going on until 9pm, with free-time in the middle of the day to work or extra tutoring if you need it. Each day will vary but there should be a rough schedule at the beginning of every week and we will try our hardest to stick with. Again, it's a mix of lectures, labs, artist talks, plus lots of time to experiment! In addition, we'll be travelling to the mountains of Kopaonik to do some investigating with nature and technology mid-program--a vacation inside of a vacation of sorts! Fun times!

Do you offer any help with obtaining a visa?

If you are selected to attend the School, we can send you an official letter of participation if that helps, but we don't have the capacity to help out much more than that. That being said, if you will require a visa, send us an email and we'll make reviewing your application a priority so do get in touch: info@schoolofma.org

Thanks! You keep saying "we", but who is organising all this?

Well, that would be me! Hi, I'm Rachel, nice to meet you:) I've been helping to organise workshops, meetups, conferences, festivals and all kinds of tech- (and non-tech) related events, for years. I've worked in audio, vfx and over the last couple of years, started learning about this magical world of art & tech.

I participated in some amazing programs in NY —  in 2013, shout out to ITP Camp and School for Poetic Computation, yo! SFPC clarified a lot and gave me direction which I can't thank them all enough for! Zach, Amit, Taeyoon, Jen and the first class of SFPC were a huge inspiration and I will try to live up to that in whatever it is the School of Machines, Making & Make-Believe becomes! Honestly, I was thinking about something like this even before then. Participating in those programs just helped me solidify in my mind what I want to do for the rest of my life: learn, and play forever with machines, making and make-believe!

I wanted to bring the same kind of experience to Europe. This year in 2015, we're organising a one-month program called 'Fabricating Empathy' in Belgrade, Serbia in Spring and a three-month summer program in Berlin (more details to come). Needless to say, we do hope you will join us!
