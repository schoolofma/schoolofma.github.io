FAQ

If you have a question and don't see it answered here, send us an email and we can add it to this section.

Great, I'm excited! How does the admissions process work?

Once you've applied to School of MA, your application will be reviewed. Within a few days, you'll receive an e-mail from us with follow-up questions. In order to keep the process moving, we ask you to please reply within 3 working days. Once we hear back from you, we'll schedule a Skype call. The call is a chance to get to know you better and for you to ask questions about the program. Within 3 days after the Skype call you'll know if you're in! If you are, we ask that you transfer a non-refundable deposit to us (within one week), to confirm you participation. Pretty standard procedure really. Questions? Email us!

This rolling admissions process is intended to make it easier for those of you travelling from abroad and needing to make plans and travel arrangements.

The School sounds like a perfect fit for me! How much does it cost?

Tuition is:
€3999* early bird (before April 20)
€4399* full-price tuition for our full-time, 10-week summer program
*Prices include mandatory 19% VAT.

We highly suggest you apply before April 20th! Applying early not only helps us to plan, it helps you out, too: earlybird discounted price is a €400 savings. The enrollment period closes for good on June 1st, or once we're full, whichever comes first.

We will be accepting only 10 students per track. Be one of the lucky ones!

What does the cost cover?

The money we receive will go entirely back into the school to pay for our wonderful facility N.K., brilliant instructors, admin, taxes, etc. We will also cover the cost of basic materials and components for the classroom, as much as we can anyway, until the money runs out!

What does the tuition NOT cover?

The tuition does not cover housing or travel to/from Berlin. It is a relatively inexpensive city, though. You can often find a room for about €300/mo. We're happy to help spread the word if you'll be needing a place. Not one of our students has ended up homeless so far, not to our knowledge anyway. Haha. No, don't worry, you will not be homeless!

I'm interested but don't think I can afford it. Do you offer assistance?

At this time, we currently do not have any means to provide financial assistnace. But if you're really interested in attending, we encourage you to be proactive about your education. Students in the past have been quite resourceful. Ask your company if they can help sponsor you, pointing out that the skills you learn will benefit them also in the end. If you're a student, ask your student advisor. Often times schools have funds allocated for additional education expenses for students. We've also heard, sometimes banks offer scholarships for students to travel abroad in summer. Bottom line is, it doesn't hurt to ask!

Other options are, crowdfunding. There are various platforms. Tilt is one we've heard about but there are of course many others!

What about sponsors?

If we find paying sponsors and all other costs are covered, we will bring the price of tuition down or refund some money. That being said, if you know of any glorious people willing to help fund us — by all means — give them my number! +49 151 753 74 602

Cool. Any final words on finances?

We've tried to keep the costs down as much as humanly possible! In an attempt to be transparent, we will be posting all of our finances online. That being said, you're not just paying for classes. The money is being used to allow an amazing community of people to come together, all of whom are passionate about dreaming up ideas and seeing how far they can push the technology, the art and themselves. It's a unique opportunity to collaborate with and learn from everyone participating and to create something amazing from nothing. Even in a parallel universe I think it would cost €3999, if not more! But good luck getting a cheap ticket to that place.

Thanks for answering my money questions, now about the School. Can I attend part-time?

The program requires a full-time commitment and we are looking for people who can dedicate the full ten weeks to it.

What kind of students are you looking for?

The curious and inquisitive kind, creative people or the kind of people who yearn to be creative. We, also really like nice people and those who enjoy helping others and sharing knowledge. Hi-five if this sounds like you!

More specifically, this summer program is a bit focused on coding, so if you're just learning how to code or interested in improving your coding skills and getting more into processing or p5.js (and other frameworks), this program is for you!

Do I need to know how to code before signing up?

No, but you can! We don't expect any prior knowledge but if you do have some, you may be able to push yourself further much faster. We have some really great instructors and no doubt you will learn a lot from them and from the other students, regardless of your current skill level. This is not about being the smartest, it's about learning to expose your creative innards to the world in your own unique way and working with new tools and people who can help make it happen.

You say the program is full-time, what does that mean exactly?

There will be a morning check-in beginning at 10am, and one main class a day Mo-Thurs. (one for each track) with a break for lunchtime of course. There will be an electronics and sensors class once a week and of course free-time each day to work or extra tutoring if you need it. Sometimes we'll have visitors and guest speakers and field trips! Each day will vary but there should be a rough schedule at the beginning of every week and we will try our hardest to stick with. Again, it's a mix of lectures, labs, artist talks, plus lots of time to experiment! Fun times!

Both tracks sound exciting! Can I take both?

If you've already got a little experience with coding and feel confident that you can handle it, we'll consider it. It's definitely doable but perhaps not recommended. In order for you to get the most out of the program or even in general, never underestimate the power of focusing on one thing at a time! But if you're really hellbent on it, let's talk!

Do you offer any help with obtaining a visa?

If you are selected to attend the School, we can send you an official letter of participation if that helps, but we don't have the capacity to help out much more than that. That being said, if you will require a visa, send us an email and we'll make reviewing your application a priority so do get in touch: info@schoolofma.org
